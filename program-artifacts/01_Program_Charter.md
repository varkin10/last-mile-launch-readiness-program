# Program Charter — Last Mile Launch Readiness Program

**Program Sponsor:** VP, Last Mile Operations
**Program Owner:** Launch Manager
**Document Status:** Approved for Execution
**Version:** 1.0 — As of August 2026

---

## 1. Business Objective

Launch 5 new Last Mile delivery stations (DXP1–DXP5) across four regions between August and September 2026, on schedule and at Day 1 operational readiness, while establishing a standardized, repeatable launch operating model that reduces launch risk and cycle time for all future site openings.

Today, station launches are coordinated ad hoc across Operations, HR, Training, Technology, Facilities, Safety, Security, Transportation, Finance, and Vendor Management using spreadsheets, email threads, and status meetings. This creates inconsistent readiness assessments, late risk discovery, and launch delays. This program exists to fix that by giving every site a single, standardized readiness process and a single source of truth for program health.

## 2. Scope

**In scope:**
- End-to-end launch coordination for 5 simulated delivery stations across 9 standardized launch phases (Site Planning through Post-Launch Stabilization)
- Cross-functional governance (RACI, RAID, stakeholder cadence) across 9 operating departments
- Day 1 operational validation protocol and Go/No-Go decision process
- Weekly Business Review (WBR) reporting to senior leadership
- Post-launch retrospective and continuous improvement capture

**Out of scope:**
- Site selection / real estate negotiation (assumed complete before program start)
- Long-term facility maintenance after 30-day post-launch stabilization
- Compensation and benefits design (HR policy, not launch execution)

## 3. Assumptions

- Each site has a signed lease and approved headcount plan before entering Site Planning
- Regional labor markets can support the hiring plan within the launch timeline
- Standard equipment and technology packages are used across all 5 sites (no site-specific technology builds)
- Vendors have existing master agreements in place; this program manages delivery execution, not contract negotiation

## 4. Constraints

- Fixed launch dates per site, driven by peak-season network capacity planning — dates do not move without VP-level approval
- Shared regional resources (IT engineers, safety auditors) support multiple concurrent launches, creating scheduling contention
- Capital budget is fixed per site at program approval; overages require Finance escalation

## 6. Success Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Sites launched on original committed date | ≥ 4 of 5 |
| Average launch readiness score at T-7 days | ≥ 85 |
| Critical (high-severity) risks open at Day 1 | 0 |
| Day 1 validation pass rate | 100% before Go decision |
| Schedule variance across all phases (program average) | ≤ 5 days |
| Post-launch issues requiring escalation (first 30 days) | ≤ 3 per site |

## 7. Top Program Risks

1. Shared regional resource contention across concurrent launches (Technology, Safety) delaying multiple sites simultaneously
2. Vendor equipment delivery delays (scanners, racking) with limited buffer before Day 1
3. Hiring pipeline shortfalls in tight regional labor markets
4. Facilities build-out delays cascading into technology install and operational testing windows

Full detail in the RAID Log (`03_RAID_Log.xlsx`).

## 9. Operating Cadence

| Cadence | Audience | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Daily stand-up (per site, final 3 weeks pre-launch) | Site Leader + department leads | Blocker resolution, task-level status |
| Weekly cross-functional launch sync | All department leads | Cross-site coordination, dependency management |
| Weekly Business Review (WBR) | Regional Ops Director, VP Last Mile Ops | Program health, escalations, decisions needed |
| Go/No-Go review (T-7 days) | Launch Manager, Site Leader, all department leads | Formal readiness decision |
| Post-launch retrospective (T+30 days) | All stakeholders | Lessons learned, process standardization |
