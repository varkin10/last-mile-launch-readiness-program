## LinkedIn Project Post

I built a Last Mile Launch Readiness Program — a full operating model for standing up 5 concurrent delivery station launches, the way I'd approach it as a Launch/Program Manager.

It's not a piece of software — it's a program: a charter, a standardized 9-phase launch playbook with entry/exit criteria and quality gates, a RACI matrix and RAID log across 9 departments, a readiness/risk/confidence scoring model that drives an objective Go/No-Go decision, a Weekly Business Review format for leadership, a Day 1 operational validation checklist, and a post-launch retrospective process that feeds lessons learned back into the playbook.

A lightweight dashboard sits on top so the readiness data is explorable, but the artifacts are the point: the same documents and governance structure a real launch program runs on.

Across the 5 simulated launches, the readiness model surfaced real risk early — concentrated technology delays and schedule slippage at several sites — well before the week of Day 1, with clear ownership assigned through the RAID log.

Happy to walk anyone through the readiness scoring logic or the playbook structure — feedback from anyone who's run launches at scale very welcome.
